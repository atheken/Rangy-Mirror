Preamble: These may well be the best two defences in the tournament – excluding gravediggers such as Switzerland, who set out en masse to kill games – and a draw would usher them both into the next round. So does that mean we're destined for the deadest of rubbers? Surely not, because there is a mighty big carrot for the team that tops this group – or rather, a big non-stick: the group winners will avoid Spain or Chile, and have the pleasure of giving the Swiss what for.

Julio Baptista replaces Kaka, as you can see form the teams below, but more intriguingly, it looks like Portugal are going to stick Cristiano Ronaldo up front. That's an interesting move by Carlos Queiroz - he's done it before but not to particularly pleasing effect and his decision to revert to it comes, curiously, after the team finally seeemd to have solved its scoring problems by recording the biggest win in their history. Queiroz seems to be suggesting that they should have hit more than seven against North Korea. That's the spirit!
